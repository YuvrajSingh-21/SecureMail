Responsible Disclosure

We deeply value the work of the security research community.

To the Security Community

At SecureMail, we understand that building a secure platform requires collaboration. We want to explicitly thank independent security researchers who dedicate their time to making the internet safer. If you believe you have found a security vulnerability in our platform, we encourage you to report it to us immediately.

How to Report

Please email your findings to security@securemail.com. We highly recommend encrypting sensitive information.

Our Expectations

  • Provide detailed steps to reproduce the vulnerability, including any relevant scripts or screenshots.
  • Make a good faith effort to avoid privacy violations, data destruction, and interruption or degradation of our service.
  • Give us a reasonable amount of time to resolve the issue before disclosing it to the public.

What Should Not Be Tested

Please refrain from testing the following:

  • Denial of Service (DoS/DDoS) attacks against our infrastructure.
  • Social engineering or phishing of SecureMail employees.
  • Physical attacks against our property or data centers.
  • Vulnerabilities in third-party integrations (like Google OAuth) that are not caused by our implementation.

Our Response Process

We aim to acknowledge receipt of vulnerability reports within 48 hours. Our security engineering team will investigate the issue and keep you updated on the progress of remediation. We commit to coordinated disclosure once the vulnerability has been patched.
