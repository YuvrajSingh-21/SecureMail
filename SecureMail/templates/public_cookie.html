Cookie Policy

How and why we use cookies on our platform. Last updated: July 2026.

1. What are cookies?

Cookies are small text files stored on your device when you visit websites. At SecureMail, we believe in radical minimalization. We only use cookies when absolutely necessary to provide you with a secure and functional experience.

2. Authentication & Session Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary. They allow you to log in securely, navigate the dashboard, and access your protected email environment. Without these cookies, the SecureMail platform cannot function. They are automatically removed or invalidated when you log out.

3. Preference Cookies

We use local storage (which functions similarly to cookies) to remember your UI preferences, such as Dark Mode toggles or sidebar collapse states. These never leave your device.

4. Analytics Cookies

Currently, we do not use third-party analytics cookies on the authenticated dashboard. If we introduce privacy-respecting analytics in the future to understand platform usage, we will update this policy and provide an explicit opt-in mechanism.

5. Managing Cookies

You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent. However, if you do not accept authentication cookies, you will not be able to use the SecureMail dashboard.
